Cream Puffs

  • FILLING
  • Warm 1 1/2 cups of WHOLE MILK in a saucepan
  • In a bowl, whisk 1/2 cup of SUGAR, 1/4 cup of FLOUR, and a dash of SALT. Then, whisk in yolks from 4 EGGS
  • Slowly, add the hot milk into this mixture, and pour this back in to the saucepan
  • Whisk constantly on medium heat until thick
  • Strain and add 1 tsp VANILLA EXTRACT
  • Cover this tightly and refridgerate until cool. You have now finished the filling.
  • PUFF BASE
  • Heat 1 stick of BUTTER, 1 tsp SUGAR, and a pinch of SALT with 1 cup of WATER to a bowl in a saucepan
  • Add 1 cup of FLOUR and stir with a wooden spoon until a film forms on the pan
  • Remove from heat and cool for 3 minutes, then add 4 EGGS, 1 at a time
  • Pipe onto a pan in little dots and tap the tips down with a wet finger
  • Brush the tops with an egg wash (1 EGG and 1 tbsp WATER)
  • Bake at 375 degrees Fahrenheit for 30 mins or until browned
  • Once cooled, fill the puffs with the filling and ENJOY!

Green Goddess Salad

  • DRESSING
  • In a blender or food processor, blend:
    • 2/3 cup of greek yogurt
    • 1/4 cup of cilantro
    • 2 scallions (or just a bit of an onion)
    • 1/4 cup lime juice
    • 1/2 cup olive oil
    • 1/2 a jalapeño (or more, depending on spice level)
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1 tbsp raw honey
  • You now have made the dressing
  • This can be kept in an air-tight container for around a week (no longer)
  • SALAD BASE
  • Gather the following ingredients:
    • Corn
    • Shredded romaine (or I sometimes use kale)
    • Cooked black beans
    • Chopped tomatoes
    • Diced scallions
    • Avocado
    • Cilantro
    • Optional protein: quinoa or meat of choice
  • Using your judgement, add the ingredients above to your bowl in the proportions of your choosing
  • Drizzle the dressing and mix and ENJOY!

Donuts

  • Whisk 1 cup of warmed MILK, 1 tbsp dry active YEAST, and 1/3 cup of granulated SUGAR in a bowl and let sit, covered for around 4 minutes or until the mixture is foamy
  • Whisk in 2 large EGGS, 6 tbsp of melted BUTTER, 1 tsp VANILLA extract, 1/2 a cup of FLOUR, and a dash of NUTMEG and SALT
  • Add 3 1/2 cups of FLOUR and mix until the dough comes together but is still sticky (add more for consistency if needed)
  • Cover the dough and let it rise until doubled in size (around 1.5 to 2 hours)
  • Roll out and cut the dough into desired shape
  • Heat oil of your choosing to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (HINT** If you do not have a thermometer, you can tell the oil is ready when a wooden stick or spoon causes a slight boil when placed in the oil)
  • Fry the donuts using your judgement for cooking time on each side (it shouldn't be long)
  • Decorate with whatever toppings you would like (I prefer cinnamon sugar) and ENJOY

Focaccia

  • Whisk 1 cup WATER, 2 tsp SUGAR, and 2 tsp YEAST together in a bowl and let rest for 5 minutes
  • Add 1 more cup of WATER, 1/4 cup OLIVE OIL, and 1 cup FLOUR
  • Knead together, and add up to 1/2 cup more flour if the dough is still sticking to the sides of the bowl
  • Continue to knead on a floured surface for around 5 minutes until a poked hole bounces back
  • Leave the dough to rise for around 2-3 hours
  • Oil the desired baking pan and flatten the dough to release air bubbles
  • Let the dough sit for another hour+ in the refrigerator
  • Pour 3 tbsp OLIVE OIL and desired toppings and HERBS on the dough and press them in with your fingers
  • Bake at 450 degrees Fahrenheit for 20 minutes and ENJOY!

Gnocchi

  • Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit
  • Bake 2 large POTATOES for an hour
  • Strain the insides of the potatoes into a bowl
  • Mix in 3/4 a cup of FLOUR and 1 EGG
  • Be careful not to knead too much
  • Roll the dough into a skinny log and cut into pieces
  • Boil the pieces until they float
  • Cook and serve with desired toppings and sauces (see below for a great combo idea) and ENJOY!

The Best Pink Pasta

  • Boil your PASTA noodles or gnocchi in salt water
  • Meanwhile, sautée GARLIC, black PEPPER, diced ONION, and TOMATOES in a pan
  • Mix in some tomato paste, a splash of pasta water, and a few scoops of any SPINACH DIP of your choosing
    • It does not have to be spinach dip, but I like using Trader Joe's Greek Yogurt Spinach and Artichoke Dip for some healthy fats and creaminess
  • Drain the rest of your pasta and add to the sauce
  • Season to your liking and don't forget RED PEPPER FLAKES for a little kick and ENJOY!

Spaghetti Squash

  • Wash a large spaghetti SQUASH and carefully slice it down the center
  • On a baking sheet, cover the squash with some OIL, BLACK PEPPER, and SALT
  • Place the squash in the oven for 40 minutes at 425 degrees Fahrenheit
  • Take it out of the oven and let cool for 5-10 minutes
  • With a fork, scrape at the sides to pull the spaghetti into noodle-like pieces
  • Top and serve with whatever you would like and ENJOY!
